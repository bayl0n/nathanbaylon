I've been pretty indifferent to password managers until I had to use them professionally for the first time at my internship at **Freelancer.com** where they used LastPass, which I used sparingly before for my personal use. Since then, I realised the benefits of them but never had the motivation to use them for personal use until I started working for **Coral Active**, which uses **1Password**.

## Which one should I use?

For me, I've only had experience with LastPass and 1Password, so those are one the table. However, recently, I've also gotten to know about BitWarden, which seems to be the most loved for single users i.e. people that don't need to share passwords like family accounts.

### LastPass vs. 1Password

The two have very similar user experiences but 1Password has a better interface. It is so easy to get to passwords you want and the browser extension is so much better. LastPass feels so much clunkier from my experience.
Not to mention, 1Password also has better security due to LastPass having data breaches in the past.

### BitWarden

Moving on from LastPass and 1Password, BitWarden also seems to be quite popular. It is the most affordable out of the three (you can't get cheaper than free) and it is also open source.

### Keychain

One that I forgot to mentioned which I also use without noticing that much is Apple's keychain. It's actually quite good but sadly I won't be considering it, and the reason being in typical Apple fashion - it isn't cross platform with non-Apple devices.

## Conclusion

After considering all the mentioned options, I decided to go with BitWarden. It is free for single users, it's open source and is also the cheapest option even after deciding that you want get a premium plan. After exporting my passwords from LastPass and Keychain, I've got it up and running and it's been smooth sailing since then.
